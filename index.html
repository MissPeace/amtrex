Exposing Online Tracking of American Muslims

Who could have a registry?

This project takes the cue from the recent Muslim ban. Since algorithms can express the behavior of internet users, it suggests that sensible data can be easily exploited just taking advantage of third party trackers already existing on the internet for lawful reasons. It targets website management, citizens who practice Islam, and tracking companies and aims to finalize a method to collaborate against such a danger, by assessing, as a first step, the presence of third-party tracking.

If a user frequently accesses web pages intended for an Islamic audience, an algorithm could assume the user is an active Islamic practitioner. This normally is addressed as an advertigins effect, since the 2007 institutions address the phenomena, and recently new progress has been made by Stanford and Princetor researcher.

Now is it clear the impact is not just on advertising business, but on the life of a category actively threatened by discriminatory policies.

Who can do such monitoring? the website itself, but when you are connecting to a single website, truly, your device is connecting to many websites. They take name of web trackers and they can monitor your navigation across many websites.

When this happen, the assumption that you are muslim, became stronger.

Websites use trackers in their pages for different, and justified, reasons: advertising, analytics, social engagement. Just, considering the muslimban, a new sensibility is necessary by websites and by trackers. This campaign contains suggestions, below, that only your crowd can ask!

These websites are ultimately responsible of the trackers served to their readers. Often they are not aware of which trackers they are including.

What we request from the tracking companies

The companies have only a partial fault, they are in a Nation in which via gag order they can be forced to cooperate, these are not polished as EFF made when DJT was still president Elected.

  • How easy is it to link the collected profiles to a phone number (and to a physical ID)? Can you minimize your data collected into groups of users instead of keeping personal behavior's history? We will assume you are keeping individual personal profile unless otherwise stated.
  • The third party inclusions present in such website create sensitive data; Please treat all users navigating in those sites as if they have Do Not Track enabled.
  • Can you disclose the amount and the kind of metadata you collect? considering these data describe societal behavior, knowing who has more metadata is in the public interest.
  • If your company, ever got any request of user selection based on their religious beliefs: send a tip off to the investigative journalists of ProPublica, they wrote this story on racial profiling perfomed by Facebook: but how many other companies can do the same?
  • Have you ever been compromised? do you have any mechanism that might prevent unfaithful employee or remote attacker to steal profile based data from your server? We have to assume you are not protected if not otherwise stated.
